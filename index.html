What Is a Medical Spa?

A medical spa, or med spa, is a hybrid between a traditional day spa and a medical clinic. It combines the relaxation and pampering of a spa with the medical expertise and advanced treatments typically found in a clinical setting.     Frequently Asked Questions

    A: Botox and dermal fillers are both popular non-surgical treatments, but they serve different purposes. Botox is a neuromodulator that temporarily relaxes facial muscles to reduce dynamic wrinkles caused by repetitive movements, such as frown lines and crow's feet. In contrast, dermal fillers add volume to areas that have lost fullness due to aging or other factors, such as the cheeks, lips, and under-eye hollows. While Botox addresses wrinkles caused by muscle movement, fillers restore lost volume and smooth out static wrinkles.
    A: The longevity of results varies depending on the treatment and individual factors. Botox typically lasts between 3 to 4 months, after which muscle activity gradually returns, and wrinkles may reappear. Dermal fillers, on the other hand, can last anywhere from 6 months to over a year, depending on the type of filler used and the area treated. For both treatments, maintenance sessions are recommended to sustain desired results.
    A: Both Botox and dermal fillers are generally considered safe when administered by qualified professionals. However, as with any medical procedure, there are potential risks and side effects. Common side effects include mild redness, swelling, or bruising at the injection sites, which typically resolve within a few days. Serious complications are rare but can include allergic reactions or infection. It's essential to consult with a licensed practitioner to discuss any concerns and ensure the treatments are appropriate for your individual needs.
